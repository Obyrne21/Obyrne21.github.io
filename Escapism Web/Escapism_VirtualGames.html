Virtual Games

Escapism

In today's society people use virtual gaming to escape from the real world. Video games first came out in 1970s and have been getting more popular since. Have you ever had a day where you just need to get away, well studies show that about 1.2 billion people play video games and about 700 million are playing online nowadays. Many of these people are playing to get away from there problems and have a time to unload. People now are playing video games as a job and are getting paid about 226,000 or 454,000 over a course of a couple years. They say that a job, like playing video games is a life of escapism from the real jobs but it's still a career. Playing video games shows it can be a stress reliever giving you that free feeling from the world's problems. When your playing video games Dopamines are released because of the excitement and deepness into the game, it is a big part in why many people have that escape. The new VR gaming, which stands for virtual reality. A headset is needed and makes you feel as if you are right in the game and you are that character. Like I said before you get that deepness in the game. My experiences with video games has been amazing, i've been playing them since I was five. They make life exciting and have distracted me from all my problems for enough time to get through it. There are all sorts of different types of games out there, shooting games, sports games, puzzles games etc... but once you find the games you like your life will become a lot better and have a time to Escape from your problems.

Definition

es·cap·ism

/əˈskāpˌizəm/

noun

the tendency to seek distraction and relief from unpleasant realities, especially by seeking entertainment or engaging in fantasy.

synonyms: fantasy, fantasizing, daydreaming, daydreams, reverie
